Economic Thinking Fundamentals

CPV Triangle

The CPV Triangle is an important mental model when it comes to Economic Thinking. Transactions include both a buyer’s and a seller’s profit - and be mutually beneficial to both parties.

Price is represented by a pendulum as price fluctuates based on market conditions and seller’s pricing decisions.

*C is Cost *P is Profit *V is Value

Left-side correlates to “Pricing & Profit”.

Price – COGS = Profit (Seller’s Profit)

Economic Concepts

Opportunity Cost

Opportunity cost is the value of the most valuable alternative that must be forgone to undertake a given act. In decision making, we must look at opportunity cost rather than book or sunk costs. That is, we must look forward rather than backward. Understanding and considering opportunity cost is critical to understanding the true cost of any given action.

Sunk Cost

Sunk costs are unrecoverable past expenditures. Because these past expenditures (time, money, toil, etc.) cannot be recovered, good economic thinkers ignore them when determining what to do going forward.

Marginal Analysis

Marginal analysis is examining the additional benefits compared to the additional costs of adding to or subtracting from the current state (after optimizing the base case).
